# Social Media Checklist for College Students

This guide outlines the essential "Do’s and Don’ts" for maintaining a responsible and professional digital presence during your college years.

---

## The Checklist

| Category | Recommendation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Do** | **Audit Privacy Settings:** Regularly check your visibility settings on all platforms to ensure personal posts stay private. |
| **Do** | **Build a Professional Brand:** Use sites like LinkedIn or a personal portfolio to highlight academic projects and volunteer work. |
| **Do** | **The "Grandmother" Test:** Before posting, ask yourself if you would be comfortable with a grandparent or a future employer seeing it. |
| **Do** | **Verify Information:** Practice digital literacy by fact-checking news or viral claims before sharing them with your network. |
| **Do** | **Network Meaningfully:** Connect with alumni, professors, and industry professionals to expand your career opportunities. |
| **Don't** | **Post Policy Violations:** Avoid sharing content that depicts illegal acts, underage drinking, or violations of university code. |
| **Don't** | **Vent Publicly:** Never post heat-of-the-moment complaints about professors, employers, or specific classes. |
| **Don't** | **Share Real-Time Location:** For personal safety, avoid tagging your exact location or posting your daily class schedule publicly. |
| **Don't** | **Engage in Negativity:** Avoid digital "flame wars," cyberbullying, or aggressive arguments that can haunt your digital footprint. |
| **Don't** | **Trust the "Delete" Button:** Treat every post as permanent. Screenshots and internet archives can preserve content long after you delete it. |

---

## Pro-Tip: Digital Wellness
While maintaining a professional image is important, don't forget to **unplug**. Managing your screen time is just as vital for your academic success and mental health as managing your privacy settings.
